Cookies Policy

The only cookies on this page are the digital kind — here's exactly how we use them.

Last updated: 16 July 2026

This Cookies Policy explains what cookies are, which cookies Pure Taste Table ("we", "us", "our") uses on https://www.puretastetable.com, why we use them, and how you can control them. This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your computer or mobile device when you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, to remember your preferences, and to provide site owners with anonymised information about how their site is used. Cookies set by us are called "first-party cookies"; cookies set by our partners (such as Google) are "third-party cookies".

2. Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly necessary cookies

These are required for the basic operation of the website (for example, remembering a preference during your visit). They do not track you across other sites and cannot be switched off in our systems.

2.2 Analytics cookies (Google Analytics 4)

We use Google Analytics 4 to understand how visitors use the Site — which pages are popular, how long people stay, and roughly where visits come from. Typical cookies include:

  • _ga — distinguishes unique visitors (expires after 2 years);
  • _ga_<container-id> — maintains session state (expires after 2 years).

The information collected is aggregated and does not directly identify you. You can opt out at any time with the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.

2.3 Advertising cookies

If advertising is displayed on this Site (for example, through Google's advertising services), third-party vendors, including Google, may use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to this website or other websites. These may include cookies used for frequency capping, ad measurement, and — where you have consented — ad personalisation.

  • Opt out of personalised advertising from Google: adssettings.google.com
  • Opt out of many other vendors' personalised ads: aboutads.info/choices or youronlinechoices.com (EU)

3. Your Consent

On your first visit, a consent banner appears at the bottom of the page asking whether you accept analytics and advertising cookies. Non-essential cookies stay switched off (via Google Consent Mode) until you press "Accept all". If you choose "Necessary only", analytics and advertising storage remain disabled. Your choice is remembered on your device; to change it later, simply clear this site's data in your browser and the banner will appear again on your next visit.

4. How to Control or Delete Cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings at any time. You can delete all cookies already on your device and set most browsers to prevent them from being placed. Instructions for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Please note that if you block all cookies, some parts of this and other websites may not function as intended.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookies Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law, or the services we use. The latest version will always be available on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.

6. Contact Us

If you have questions about our use of cookies, contact us at hello@puretastetable.com or via our Contact page.
